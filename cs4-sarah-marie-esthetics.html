The Starting Point

Sarah Marie came to this engagement with something many early-stage business owners don't have: a real skill, a defined point of view, and clients who already trusted her. She was a licensed esthetician with an active Instagram following, a reputation for genuine skincare education, and a growing demand for her consultations and services.

What she didn't have was structure. No written document explained what the business was, what it offered, or how it was supposed to grow. Services weren't formally defined. Content happened when it happened. There was no way to track whether the business was actually moving in the right direction — no 90-day plan, no brand guide, no performance dashboard, no client acquisition system.

The gap between where the practice was and where she wanted it to go wasn't about skill or work ethic. It was the absence of a foundation. This engagement was about building one — across every area of the business at once, in a way that all fit together.

"I knew what I wanted to build. I just needed someone to help me put it together in a way that made sense — and that I could actually follow."

The Client Journey We Designed Around

Every piece of the engagement was built around a clear, documented client acquisition flow — from first discovery on Instagram to long-term client relationship. The strategy, content plan, and tech stack were all designed to move people through this journey consistently.

Scope of the Engagement

Five interconnected deliverables — each designed to work with the others. The brand guide informed the content plan. The business strategy shaped the 90-day plan. The KPI dashboard tracked the metrics defined in the strategy. Nothing was built in isolation.

Business Strategy

Business Strategy Overview — 11 pages

Business overview, positioning, core service definition, revenue model (consultations, esthetician services, affiliate product recommendations), client journey, marketing strategy, business system, technology stack, key metrics, financial projections by month, and long-term vision. This became the master reference document for every other piece of the engagement.

Brand Foundation

Branding Guide

Complete visual and tonal identity: color palette (Blush Pink, Sand, Warm Taupe, Amethyst Smoke, Sage), typography system (Fraunes / Kulachat Serif / Allura), visual direction principles, mood board, and Instagram profile sample board. Designed to be the reference point for all future content creation — by Sarah Marie or anyone supporting her.

Execution Planning

90-Day Focused Launch Plan

A fully phased plan across three stages: Foundation & Setup (Days 1–30), Growth & Consistency (Days 31–60), and Conversion & Optimization (Days 61–90). Each phase included specific launch priorities, a week-by-week rollout with named action items, measurable success indicators, and a complete setup checklist covering both what to create and what to configure technically.

Content System

Content Launch Plan

A 30-day Instagram content roadmap built around four defined content pillars: Day in the Life, Routine & Products, Client Interaction, and Skincare Education. Included post format, theme, and sequence for each day — plus a full story highlight structure, repeatable story formats, and a complete outline for the free skincare guide that anchors the entire client acquisition funnel.

KPI Dashboard

Custom Excel Dashboard

Tracking five metrics from the business strategy: guide downloads, consultation bookings, service appointments, return client rate, and monthly revenue. Built to be updated weekly with data already available to the client, with a summary view for quick review and trend tracking over time. No new software required.

What This Engagement Required

This project required holding creative and operational complexity at the same time. Brand identity, visual direction, content strategy, and voice on one side — business documentation, phased planning, metric selection, and dashboard design on the other. Both had to be done well for the whole to function.

The client was a solo practitioner with no administrative support and limited bandwidth for complexity. Every deliverable was built with that reality in mind — clear enough to be used without guidance, structured enough to be built on over time, and specific enough to actually drive decisions. The 90-day plan in particular required translating a broad vision into concrete weekly actions that a one-person operation could realistically sustain alongside existing client work.
